# Research Patterns

Use these checklists to adapt `hermes-deep-research` to the topic.

## General Topic / Landscape
Cover:
- broad exploration
- subquestion decomposition
- source diversity
- contradictions mapping
- recommendation only if evidence supports one

## Comparison (X vs Y)
Always cover:
- strengths of X
- strengths of Y
- where each wins
- hidden tradeoffs
- migration / switching costs
- recommendation by use-case, not one-size-fits-all

## Market / Company / Vendor Diligence
Always cover:
- what the company or product claims
- what independent sources confirm
- pricing and business model if relevant
- adoption and customer evidence if available
- key risks / criticisms / lock-in / fragility
- what would falsify the bull case

## GitHub / OSS Due Diligence
Prioritize:
- official repo and docs
- recent commit activity
- releases and maintenance signals
- issue / PR patterns
- contributor concentration risk
- architecture and dependencies
- community adoption and ecosystem fit

For GitHub/OSS tasks, start with official repo/docs before secondary commentary.

## Decision Memo
Prioritize:
- recommendation first
- reasons with evidence
- tradeoffs and switching costs
- what would change the conclusion

## Scout Brief
Prioritize:
- only the highest-signal findings
- inline URLs next to each item
- no separate sources section unless explicitly requested
- compact, chat-native structure
